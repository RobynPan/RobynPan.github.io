# Bearcover Robot

tags: Robotics, Healthcare, Radar, Berlin, Startup, Care Tech

Oscar is a care assistant robot developed at Bearcover GmbH — a Berlin-based startup I co-founded. He lives in nursing homes and hospitals and checks on patients and residents during the night shift, without ever entering their rooms.

Using radar sensors, Oscar can detect breathing, movement, and presence through walls and closed doors. No cameras. No microphones. No sensitive data leaves the room. The goal is to relieve night-shift nursing staff from constant manual rounds while actually improving the quality of care.

## The problem

Night-shift care is one of the most strained parts of the healthcare system. A single nurse may be responsible for dozens of residents, doing manual wellness checks every hour — walking room to room, opening doors, risking disturbing sleep. It is exhausting, and the gaps between checks are long enough that incidents go undetected.

## The solution

Oscar moves through corridors autonomously and uses Doppler radar to monitor each room remotely. If something is out of the ordinary — no movement, irregular breathing, a fall — he flags it immediately. Staff are alerted only when they're needed.

## Technology

- Doppler radar sensors (no optical data)
- Autonomous navigation on flat corridor floors
- Custom alert and dashboard software
- Supported by Michels Bernau and Motion Lab Berlin
