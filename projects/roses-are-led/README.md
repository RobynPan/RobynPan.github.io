# Roses Are LED

tags: Electronics, Art, Solar, Phosphorescence, Light, HAEQS

*A Rose, to shine when the world seems Dark.*

A piece I made in the darkness of winter to expand my understanding of phosphorescent pigment. Transparent phosphorescent petals surround a UV LED powered by solar panels — the same way garden path lights work. Daylight charges the petals, which glow softly until full darkness, when the LED lights up and charges them again from the centre of the rose.

The effect is a rose that never quite goes dark. During the day it looks like a delicate, slightly eerie silk flower. At dusk the petals begin to glow on their own. Late at night, the LED pulses on and re-saturates them from the inside.
